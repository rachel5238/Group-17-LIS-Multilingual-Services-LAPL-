Annotated Bibliography

American Library Association.Guidelines for the Development and Promotion of Multilingual Collections and Services. Chicago: American Library Association. This guideline outlines professional standards for libraries that want to create multilingual collections and services. It covers best practices for collection development, community outreach, and ensuring equitable access to information for multilingual populations. This resource is helpful because it provides an official framework that librarians can follow when designing multilingual services.

LibVoices. Podcast. Hosted by Jamia Williams and Jamillah R. Gabriel. Various episodes. Started 2020. Accessed March 12, 2026. https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/libvoices/id1502263387. LibVoices is a podcast that features conversations of librarians of color, discussing career experiences, equity challenges, leadership, and community-engaged work. Its episodes help show how librarians approach diversity, representation, and inclusion; topics closely connected to multilingual services and culturally responsive practices in libraries.

Literacy Across Languages. Podcast. Hosted by Mary Sandoval and Katherine Nelson. Began 2025. Accessed March 12, 2026. https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/literacy-across-languages/id1860605501 Literacy across languages is a biweekly podcast that discussed multilingual literacy instruction and language acquisition research, offering practical strategies for supporting multilingual learners. While oriented towards educators, the conversations about inclusive language practices and culturally responsive education provide insights relevant to multilingual librarianship, especially when considering how libraries can serve linguistically diverse communities.

Long Beach City College Library Technician Program. “Library Diversity & Multilingual Collections (Guest Speakers).” YouTube video, 56:42. Posted June 15, 2021. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ikDapIcB6FQ&utm This video from the Long Beach City College Library Technician Program features librarians talking about diversity, equity, inclusion, and multilingual collections in library environments. It highlights how librarians engage with multilingual materials and community needs, which makes it a great resource for understanding real world practices in this field.

Valdovinos, Patricia. “Author Page.”Los Angeles Public Library. Accessed March 12, 2026. https://www.lapl.org/collections-resources/blogs/lapl/authors/pvaldovinos The Los Angeles Public Library blog page for Patricia Valdovinos highlights posts focused on multilingual collections, reading recommendations, and resources for Spanish-speaking and multilingual communities. The blog provides insight into how libraries curate culturally representative materials and promote multilanguage literature to the public. This resource is helpful because it shows how librarians actively engage with communities and promote diverse collections through outreach and digital content.
